# PoS Version Comparison

## Summary
This document is a quick comparison note covering Orbs PoS V1, V2, V2.5, and V3. It prioritizes official public materials, but some dates and labels are described slightly differently across official posts, so a final standardized timeline still requires further verification.

## High-Level Evolution
- V1: launch-era PoS structure using a hybrid approach that relied on Ethereum as an external oversight and security layer
- V2: Age of Guardians, with staking contracts fully deployed on Ethereum and stronger emphasis on the Guardian role
- V2.5: improvements around reward distribution, self-delegation, gas optimization, and the Guardian or Delegator operating model
- V3: Ethereum plus Polygon multi-chain staking, with fixed APR references and broader structural adjustments

## Comparison Table
| Version | Core Positioning | Main Features | Operational / Economic Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| V1 | Mainnet launch-era PoS | Introduced the hybrid blockchain concept and used Ethereum as a base-layer oversight and security anchor | Starting point for the first year of network operations |
| V2 | Age of Guardians | Fully deployed PoS staking contracts on Ethereum and gave Guardians a more explicit operating role | Strengthened the Guardian-centered PoS model |
| V2.5 | V2 refinement | Added protocol-level automated reward distribution, Guardian control over Delegator reward splits, more flexible reward claiming, gas-cost reduction architecture, and minimum self-delegation requirements | Improved reward sustainability and operating efficiency |
| V3 | Multi-chain staking | Added Ethereum plus Polygon staking, single-validator-node multi-network support, fixed 10% APR references, and additional changes on top of the V2 model | Expanded toward lower-fee staking environments and adjusted reward allocation framing |

## Version Notes
### V1
- Appears with the 2019 mainnet launch
- Described as the early form of the hybrid blockchain design
- Official materials describe Ethereum as providing external oversight and additional security for the Orbs PoS mechanism

### V2
- Referred to as the Age of Guardians
- Repeatedly described as a model in which staking contracts were fully deployed on Ethereum
- Strengthened the role of Guardians and the economic alignment of the PoS model

### V2.5
- An important intermediate upgrade in official materials
- Introduced automated rewards distribution
- Allowed Guardians to adjust Delegator reward splits
- Allowed Delegators to choose when to claim rewards
- Moved additional PoS components onto Ethereum
- Introduced minimum self-delegation requirements
- Adopted gas-cost reduction architecture
- Sought a more sustainable reward structure

### V3
- Introduced multi-chain staking on Ethereum and Polygon
- Official FAQ and announcement materials mention a fixed 10% APR reference
- Official materials say Guardians can operate a single validator node while supporting both networks
- Self-stake requirements may still exist on a per-network basis
- There were later governance-era changes and discussions about removing or reinstating self-stake requirements

## Important Ambiguities
- Some official posts describe the V2 timing as a November 2019 launch, while broader retrospective materials frame it somewhat differently
- The boundary between V2 and V2.5 is emphasized differently across whitepapers, blogs, and operational announcements
- Post-V3 self-stake requirements should be treated as governance-history-dependent, not as one permanently fixed rule
- Lock periods, reward splits, and APR figures differ by version and should not be collapsed into a single simplified statement

## Recommended Usage
- For general introductions, use the basic V1 to V2 to V2.5 to V3 evolution narrative
- For investor or research explanations, separate reward models, self-stake rules, gas efficiency, and multi-chain scope by version
- For operational guidance, always distinguish current live rules from historical version notes
