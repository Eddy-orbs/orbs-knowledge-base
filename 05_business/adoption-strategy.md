# Adoption Strategy

## Summary
Based on current public materials, Orbs’ adoption strategy appears closer to integrating execution products into DEXs and DeFi protocols than to acquiring end users directly through standalone consumer applications.

## Strategy Components
- Integrate advanced order products into DEXs
- Become the execution layer behind improved DeFi trading UX
- Expand from spot-trading enhancements into liquidity and perpetuals infrastructure
- Extend into autonomous agent execution
- Provide direct institutional access to Orbs execution infrastructure through Orbs Institutional

## Working Interpretation
Rather than trying to own all liquidity or trading activity directly, Orbs appears to follow a B2B2C-style infrastructure strategy that upgrades the trading experience of other protocols.

## Institutional Expansion
Orbs Institutional, announced on 2026-06-11, expands the adoption narrative from DEX-integrated execution infrastructure into institution-facing execution infrastructure. It is aimed at professional trading teams, OTC desks, treasuries, custodians, wallet providers, exchanges, MPC providers, prime brokers, and other institutional platforms that want direct access to Orbs execution products.

The stack includes Liquidity Hub, dTWAP, dLIMIT, and dSLTP. Its positioning is not a separate custody product: assets remain in the user's or institution's own wallet, while Orbs provides execution infrastructure, private RFQ access through Liquidity Hub, API access, EIP-712-compatible signing support, and white-label / co-branded integration options.
