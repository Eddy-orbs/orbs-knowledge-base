# Business Model

## Summary
Based on current public materials, Orbs’ business model appears closer to providing execution infrastructure or a backend layer for DeFi protocols and DEXs than to simply marketing a chain by itself. In practical terms, Orbs appears to package advanced order logic, liquidity optimization, derivatives execution, and agent execution as products.

## Core Logic
- A DeFi protocol and DEX integration-driven model
- Layer-3 execution services as infrastructure
- A product suite centered on dLIMIT, dTWAP, dSLTP, Liquidity Hub, Perpetual Hub, and Agentic
- A network secured and economically coordinated by ORBS token incentives

## Business Interpretation
Orbs’ current direction appears closer to "not just a chain with a token" and more toward delivering execution products that can actually be integrated into live protocols. In that sense, the business narrative is explained more naturally by which DEXs or DeFi apps improve trading experience through Orbs products than by chain-level branding alone.

## Key Revenue / Value Hypotheses To Track
- Protocol integrations as a growth engine
- Value accrual through network usage and the token-aligned incentive layer
- Ecosystem expansion through products becoming a de facto execution standard

## Open Questions
- How does Orbs officially describe its revenue model?
- What is the relationship between product usage and ORBS token value accrual?
- What are the key business-development KPIs: integrations, volume, staking participation, or something else?
