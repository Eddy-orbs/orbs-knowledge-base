# Business Model

## Summary
Based on current public materials, Orbs’ business model appears closer to providing execution infrastructure or a backend layer for DeFi protocols and DEXs than to simply marketing a chain by itself. In practical terms, Orbs appears to package advanced order logic, liquidity optimization, derivatives execution, and agent execution as products.

## Core Logic
- A DeFi protocol and DEX integration-driven model
- Layer-3 execution services as infrastructure
- A product suite centered on dLIMIT, dTWAP, dSLTP, Liquidity Hub, Perpetual Hub, Agentic, and SPOT
- A network secured and economically coordinated by ORBS token incentives

## Business Interpretation
Orbs’ current direction appears closer to "not just a chain with a token" and more toward delivering execution products that can actually be integrated into live protocols. In that sense, the business narrative is explained more naturally by which DEXs or DeFi apps improve trading experience through Orbs products than by chain-level branding alone.

## Key Revenue / Value Hypotheses To Track
- Protocol integrations as a growth engine
- Value accrual through network usage and the token-aligned incentive layer
- Ecosystem expansion through products becoming a de facto execution standard

## Global Operating Footprint
- Tel Aviv, Israel is the HQ / headquarters, with key executives plus operations and development teams based there.
- The UK office is in London and is mainly composed of global development team members.
- Korea and Japan cover Asia business and community management.
- See `global-offices.md` for the structured office-role summary.

## April 2026 DAO and Ecosystem Signal
The April 2026 DAO rollout adds an explicit governance and capital-allocation layer to Orbs' business narrative. Orbs publicly states that the DAO will govern protocol revenue allocation, token economics, network upgrades, Guardian/validator oversight, and ecosystem grants. This suggests that future business-model explanations should connect product usage and protocol revenue to community-directed allocation decisions.

Orbzy also provides an ecosystem-distribution signal: an independent team built a beta agentic DeFi interface on Orbs Protocol infrastructure. This supports the interpretation that Orbs' business direction is not limited to first-party DEX integrations; third-party builders can also use Orbs execution infrastructure as a backend for new trading UX surfaces.

## May 2026 SPOT Business Signal
SPOT adds a first-party agent-native distribution angle to Orbs' business narrative. Instead of only improving human-facing DEX interfaces through integrations, Orbs can now be explained as providing execution infrastructure that AI agents can read, parameterize, and submit through wallet-signable orders.

Business implications to track:
- Whether agent-native documentation and order schemas become a new distribution channel for Orbs products.
- Whether SPOT drives measurable usage of dLIMIT, dTWAP, Liquidity Hub, or related execution services.
- Whether Orbs develops explicit agent-safety guardrails such as policy limits, allowed tokens, approval scopes, or execution-risk checks.
- Whether the AI-agent narrative converts into real volume, integrations, protocol revenue, or third-party builder adoption.
