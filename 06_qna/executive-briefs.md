# Executive Briefs

## 30-second Brief
Orbs is currently positioned as a decentralized Layer-3 execution layer for DeFi. Its core value proposition is bringing CeFi-level execution to DeFi through products such as dLIMIT, dTWAP, dSLTP, Liquidity Hub, Perpetual Hub, and Agentic.

## 2-minute Brief
Orbs is a blockchain project founded in 2017 that launched its mainnet in March 2019. Today, it appears to be focused less on being a general-purpose chain and more on being DeFi execution infrastructure. The ORBS token is tied to fee settlement, staking, Guardian selection, and rewards, while the network operates through a PoS-based incentive structure. On the product side, the most visible pieces are advanced order protocols such as dLIMIT, dTWAP, and dSLTP, together with Liquidity Hub, Perpetual Hub, Orbs Agentic, and VM or Lambda execution services. On the partner side, dLIMIT and dTWAP-style integrations appear to be the broadest footprint.

## 5-minute Brief
Orbs began as a project strongly associated with PoS and hybrid architecture concepts, but its current positioning is much more clearly focused on being an execution layer for DeFi. The ORBS token is not just a passive asset. It sits at the center of staking, Guardian selection, rewards, and fee-settlement incentives. The key differentiation is not simply that Orbs provides another chain, but that it provides decentralized backend infrastructure for complex trading and execution logic that DEXs and DeFi protocols may not want to build alone. That strategy is expressed through products such as dLIMIT, dTWAP, dSLTP, Liquidity Hub, Perpetual Hub, and Agentic. Looking at the available partner inventory, dLIMIT and dTWAP-related integrations appear to be the most widespread. As a result, Orbs is better understood as part of the DeFi trading infrastructure stack than as a typical general-purpose blockchain project.
